# 知识产权法律英语 - 第一阶段：基础词汇与核心概念（扩展版）

## Lesson 1: Patent（专利）核心词汇详解

### 1.1 基础术语（Basic Terms）

| 英文术语 | 中文翻译 | 法律定义与用法 |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Patent | 专利 | A government authority or license conferring a right or title for a set period, especially the sole right to exclude others from making, using, or selling an invention. <br>**例句**: The company filed a patent application for its new technology. |
| Invention | 发明 | A new and useful process, machine, article of manufacture, or composition of matter. <br>**例句**: The invention represents a significant advancement in the field of renewable energy. |
| Inventor | 发明人 | The person or persons who contribute to the conception of the invention. <br>**例句**: The inventor must be named in the patent application. |
| Patentee | 专利权人 | The person or entity to whom a patent is granted. <br>**例句**: The patentee has exclusive rights to the patented invention. |
| Assignee | 受让人 | The person or entity to whom patent rights are transferred. <br>**例句**: The assignee acquired all rights to the patent through an assignment agreement. |
| Licensee | 被许可人 | The person or entity granted permission to use a patented invention. <br>**例句**: The licensee pays royalties to the patent holder. |
| Application | 专利申请 | A request for a patent filed with a patent office. <br>**例句**: The patent application was filed on January 15, 2024. |
| Filing Date | 申请日 | The date on which a patent application is received by the patent office. <br>**例句**: The filing date determines priority for patent protection. |
| Priority Date | 优先权日 | The date of the first filing of a patent application, used to establish priority over later filings. <br>**例句**: The priority date is based on the earlier-filed foreign application. |
| Grant | 授权 | The official act of issuing a patent. <br>**例句**: The patent was granted after examination by the patent office. |
| Issue Date | 授权日 | The date on which a patent is officially issued. <br>**例句**: The patent term begins from the issue date. |
| Publication | 公布 | The act of making a patent application available to the public. <br>**例句**: The patent publication disclosed the invention's details. |
| Examination | 审查 | The process by which a patent office reviews a patent application. <br>**例句**: The examination period typically lasts 2-3 years. |
| Examiner | 审查员 | The patent office official who reviews patent applications. <br>**例句**: The examiner issued an office action rejecting certain claims. |
| Office Action | 审查意见通知书 | A written communication from a patent examiner regarding a patent application. <br>**例句**: The applicant must respond to the office action within three months. |
| Response | 答复 | The applicant's reply to an office action. <br>**例句**: The response addressed all objections raised by the examiner. |
| Amendment | 修改 | Changes made to a patent application during prosecution. <br>**例句**: The amendment narrowed the scope of the claims. |
| Prosecution | 专利申请程序 | The process of obtaining a patent from filing to grant. <br>**例句**: Patent prosecution requires careful strategy and timing. |
| Maintenance Fee | 年费/维持费 | Fees paid to keep a patent in force. <br>**例句**: Failure to pay maintenance fees results in patent expiration. |
| Term | 期限 | The period during which a patent remains in force. <br>**例句**: The patent term is 20 years from the filing date. |
| Expiration | 到期 | The end of a patent's term. <br>**例句**: The patent expiration allows competitors to use the invention. |
| Lapse | 失效 | The termination of patent rights due to failure to pay fees or other requirements. <br>**例句**: The patent lapsed when the maintenance fee was not paid. |
| Abandonment | 放弃 | The voluntary or involuntary termination of a patent application. <br>**例句**: The applicant filed a notice of abandonment. |
| Withdrawal | 撤回 | The act of withdrawing a patent application. <br>**例句**: The applicant withdrew the application due to prior art. |
| Rejection | 驳回 | The examiner's refusal to grant a patent. <br>**例句**: The rejection was based on lack of novelty. |
| Allowance | 授权通知 | The examiner's notification that a patent will be granted. <br>**例句**: The notice of allowance was received after the final response. |

### 1.2 专利要件（Patent Requirements）

| 英文术语 | 中文翻译 | 法律定义与用法 |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Novelty | 新颖性 | The requirement that an invention must be new and not previously disclosed to the public. <br>**例句**: The invention lacks novelty because it was described in a prior publication. |
| Non-obviousness | 非显而易见性 | The requirement that an invention must not be obvious to a person having ordinary skill in the art. <br>**例句**: The combination of known features was found to be obvious. |
| Utility | 实用性 | The requirement that an invention must be useful and have practical application. <br>**例句**: The invention lacks utility because it has no practical use. |
| Enablement | 充分公开 | The requirement that a patent specification must enable a person skilled in the art to make and use the invention. <br>**例句**: The specification fails to provide enablement for the claimed invention. |
| Written Description | 书面描述 | The requirement that a patent specification must contain a written description of the invention. <br>**例句**: The written description adequately supports the claimed subject matter. |
| Best Mode | 最佳实施方式 | The requirement that the inventor must disclose the best way of carrying out the invention. <br>**例句**: The inventor failed to disclose the best mode of the invention. |
| Definiteness | 明确性 | The requirement that patent claims must be clear and definite. <br>**例句**: The claims lack definiteness because they are ambiguous. |
| Patentability | 可专利性 | The criteria that an invention must meet to be eligible for patent protection. <br>**例句**: The invention's patentability is questionable due to prior art. |
| Eligible Subject Matter | 可专利客体 | The types of inventions that can be patented. <br>**例句**: Software may be eligible subject matter if it meets certain requirements. |
| Statutory Requirement | 法定要求 | The legal requirements for patentability. <br>**例句**: The invention meets all statutory requirements. |

### 1.3 专利权利要求（Patent Claims）

| 英文术语 | 中文翻译 | 法律定义与用法 |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Claim | 权利要求 | The portion of a patent application that defines the scope of the invention. <br>**例句**: Claim 1 defines the core invention. |
| Independent Claim | 独立权利要求 | A claim that stands alone without reference to other claims. <br>**例句**: Independent claim 1 sets forth the broadest scope of protection. |
| Dependent Claim | 从属权利要求 | A claim that refers back to and further limits a preceding claim. <br>**例句**: Dependent claim 2 adds additional limitations to claim 1. |
| Scope of Claims | 权利要求范围 | The extent of protection defined by the claims. <br>**例句**: The scope of claims should be carefully drafted. |
| Claim Construction | 权利要求解释 | The process of interpreting the meaning of patent claims. <br>**例句**: Claim construction is a matter of law for the court to decide. |
| Claim Element | 权利要求要素 | A component or limitation within a claim. <br>**例句**: Each claim element must be present for infringement. |
| Preamble | 前序部分 | The introductory portion of a claim that states the purpose or context. <br>**例句**: The preamble of claim 1 describes the field of the invention. |
| Transition Phrase | 过渡短语 | Words that connect the preamble to the body of a claim. <br>**例句**: "Comprising" is an open-ended transition phrase. |
| Body of Claim | 权利要求主体 | The portion of a claim that sets forth the specific limitations. <br>**例句**: The body of claim 1 lists all essential elements. |
| Means-plus-function | 功能限定权利要求 | A claim that defines an element in terms of its function. <br>**例句**: The means-plus-function claim was construed narrowly. |

### 1.5 专利侵权（Patent Infringement）

| 英文术语 | 中文翻译 | 法律定义与用法 |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Infringement | 侵权 | The unauthorized making, using, selling, or importing of a patented invention. <br>**例句**: The defendant was found liable for patent infringement. |
| Direct Infringement | 直接侵权 | Infringement that occurs when someone performs all elements of a claim. <br>**例句**: Direct infringement requires performing every claim element. |
| Indirect Infringement | 间接侵权 | Infringement that occurs through inducement or contribution. <br>**例句**: Indirect infringement includes inducement to infringe. |
| Contributory Infringement | 帮助侵权 | Supplying a component of a patented invention knowing it will be used in infringement. <br>**例句**: The supplier was liable for contributory infringement. |
| Inducement to Infringe | 诱导侵权 | Actively encouraging someone to infringe a patent. <br>**例句**: The company was found liable for inducement to infringe. |
| Literal Infringement | 字面侵权 | Infringement where the accused product literally meets all claim limitations. <br>**例句**: The product literally infringes claim 1. |
| Doctrine of Equivalents | 等同原则 | A doctrine that extends patent protection to equivalents of claimed elements. <br>**例句**: The doctrine of equivalents was applied to find infringement. |
| Willful Infringement | 故意侵权 | Deliberate and intentional infringement of a patent. <br>**例句**: Willful infringement may result in enhanced damages. |
| Declaratory Judgment | 确认之诉 | A lawsuit seeking a court declaration of non-infringement or invalidity. <br>**例句**: The plaintiff filed a declaratory judgment action. |
| Injunction | 禁令 | A court order prohibiting infringement. <br>**例句**: The court issued a permanent injunction against the infringer. |

## Lesson 2: Trademark（商标）核心词汇详解

|---------|---------|-------------|

| Brand | 品牌 | A name, term, design, or symbol that identifies goods/services. <br>**例句**: The brand has strong consumer recognition. |

| Slogan | 口号 | A phrase used in advertising to identify a brand. <br>**例句**: The slogan is registered as a trademark. |
| Mark | 标记 | A general term for trademarks, service marks, etc. <br>**例句**: The mark is registered with the USPTO. |
| Registrant | 注册人 | The owner of a registered trademark. <br>**例句**: The registrant maintains exclusive rights to the mark. |
| Applicant | 申请人 | The person or entity applying for trademark registration. <br>**例句**: The applicant filed a trademark application. |

| Registration | 注册 | The official recording of a trademark with a trademark office. <br>**例句**: The trademark registration provides nationwide protection. |

### 2.4 商标侵权（Trademark Infringement）

| 英文术语 | 中文翻译 | 法律定义与用法 |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Infringement | 侵权 | The unauthorized use of a trademark in a way that causes confusion. <br>**例句**: The defendant was found liable for trademark infringement. |

ABSTRACT

A method and system for processing data in a distributed computing environment is disclosed. The method comprises receiving a data request from a client device, identifying a plurality of processing nodes capable of processing the data request, distributing the data request among the identified processing nodes based on workload distribution criteria, collecting processed data from the processing nodes, and transmitting the processed data to the client device. The system includes a load balancer configured to distribute data requests among processing nodes, a plurality of processing nodes configured to process data requests, and a data aggregator configured to collect and aggregate processed data from the processing nodes.

#### 专利说明书（Description）

```
DESCRIPTION

TECHNICAL FIELD

The present disclosure relates generally to distributed computing systems, and more particularly to methods and systems for processing data in a distributed computing environment.

BACKGROUND

Distributed computing environments have become increasingly important for processing large volumes of data efficiently. Traditional centralized computing systems often struggle to handle the increasing demands of modern applications, particularly those requiring real-time processing of large datasets. Distributed computing systems address this challenge by distributing processing tasks across multiple nodes, thereby improving performance and scalability.

However, existing distributed computing systems often suffer from inefficient workload distribution, leading to suboptimal performance. Some nodes may be overloaded while others remain underutilized, resulting in inefficient resource utilization. Additionally, existing systems may not adequately account for factors such as network latency, processing capacity, and priority levels when distributing workload.

Therefore, there is a need for improved methods and systems for processing data in distributed computing environments that address these and other limitations of existing approaches.

SUMMARY
